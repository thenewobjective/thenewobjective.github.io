## Bloody Harvest

I think I dreamt of the end of civilization

I see visions of passing through time in moments.

A scene of hordes of humans running through a field of wheat from an unknown danger.

The unknown danger was represented by an overlarge harvesting machine that was moving through the field from left to right.

But the field and people running through it were segregated by social class. There were three lanes of wheat that was colored from left to right: red, orange, gold. The fewest and richest on the left and poorest on the right. As the danger approached from behind the left hand side culled rightward, eliminating all the wheat and what seemed to be most of the people.

## Post-Apocalypse

Forward through time a devout Christian family survives in a pastoral lifestyle.

They feel like my family but without a blood relation. Or perhaps I am inhabiting the body of another man who is.

Winters are harsh and the plants and animals are strange. Ex: sparrows with snake heads as if the serpents evolved to take the avian role. It's a temperate climate, but seemingly very fragile.

As I stand watching I can see time begin to accelerate through the years again, I rush to the family while they still exist in hope to slow the clock as I move closer.

When I enter the house, most of the people I knew there from before are gone but a young child is now a young devout man with two new youths I do not know. I ask about the father whom they imply is no longer around and seem insulted at the question.

I meet the matron of the family in a reclining chair. She has aged decades, suffered a minor stroke and approaching her end. She alone remembers me from the years past and knows I am traveling through time.

I weep over my inability to control my travel. She comforts me. I ask her if I had the power to prevent what had happened if I should. It was an apocalypse but so much time has passed and new lives and families would then no longer exist.

She smiles and says nothing as I fall away and awake.

## Interpretation

### The Harvest

The wheat field is obviously a metaphor for the human population. I'm reminded of Pharaoh’s dream in [Genesis 41](https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-41/).

The oncoming harvester is the poorly understood apocalyptic event that will cull the population. But I don't see it portrayed as malicious. It simply harvests. That is a profound distinction.
A harvester does not "murder" wheat. It fulfills the purpose of the field. What if this is not evil, but necessity? The machine is almost an impersonal force of history. Does it represent oncoming war? Something with AI? Whatever it is, it's something vastly larger than individual human intention.

The people are running through the wheat. They are not separate from it. Psychologically they **are** the crop. It's like civilization has become identified with its own product. People have become indistinguishable from what civilization cultivates. I'm reminded of a recent Chinese slang: ["human leeks"](https://www.reddit.com/r/AskAChinese/comments/1jjauom/why_do_chinese_people_often_use_the_expression/)

The division into three lanes of wheat by social classes is not just an interesting reflection but of what happens next: as the machine moves left to right, it's the rich that strike out and
attempt to consume the neighboring lane, and then the middle lane does the same. The hierarchy is irrelevant before history. Nearly everyone is harvested.

### The Compound

The survivors reach apparent safety then disease arrives. The danger is not escaped it merely changes form. The old world has died and the future has not yet formed.

### Forward Through Time

The second half of the dream takes a very different form. The first half is collective, and
the second becomes intimate/individual. I am no longer a witness to the end of civilization but a participant in the rebuilding of it.

While the family encountered happened to be some form of Christian, I do not think that is the point. The point may simply be that the family is a microcosm of civilization. The family is the fundamental unit of society and survival. I believe they survive because they possess orientation rather than technology. There is no government, economy, nor industry. Are they the beginning of a new cycle, or are this the end of history?

### Strange animals

The snake-headed sparrows fascinated me. They are a fusion of two symbolic opposites. It's like
the [winged Ourobouros](https://www.gnosisjung.org/2025/03/carl-jung-and-ouroboros.html).

After apocalypse, nature has reorganized itself.

### Passing through time

I remain psychologically continuous while history races. I am the only stable observer.
Everything changes but my observing ego remains. I rush toward them because proximity slows time.

When I ask about the missing father, the man is offended. Why? Possibly because I am asking from the wrong temporal frame. To me he was just alive. To them he has long become an ancestor.

I suspect the old woman functions as the [Wise Old Woman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wise_Old_Man_and_Wise_Old_Woman) archetype ([Sophia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sophia_(Gnosticism)#Jungian_views)).

She alone remembers both timelines, recognizes my nature, understands my suffering, and offers no argument. She simply knows.

### The Central Question

The dream ends with a question: if I had the power to prevent what had happened, should I?

Notice this is not asking: "Can I?", but instead "Should I?". That is an ethical question which the dream figure refuses to answer, or rather answers with silence. The dream is not about the end of civilization, but what? The inevitability of change and the ethical implications of intervention? Was I asking the wrong kind of question. Or perhaps life cannot be evaluated from outside history.

Both choices destroy lives. Even if I had the hubris to think I could prevent the cataclysm, there is no clean moral solution. The dream refuses simplistic heroism. Why did I assume I was in any position to intervene? What responsibility do I hold?
